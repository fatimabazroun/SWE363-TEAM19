Seven Lost Pearls of the Arabian Gulf

Written by: Omar Al-Faris Published by: Yazeed Alghamdi Genre: Fiction Region of Origin: Riyadh Word Count: 504

Explore the magic of Saudi folklore as we uncover the legend of the Seven Lost Pearls of the Arabian Gulf. It is said that long ago, seven pearls of unimaginable beauty were gifted by the sea to a noble tribe that dwelled along the coast of what is now Saudi Arabia. These pearls were not merely treasures; they held the wisdom of the ocean, granting prosperity, protection, and harmony to those who possessed them. But greed and jealousy know no bounds. As rival tribes heard whispers of the pearls' power, war broke out, and one by one, the pearls were lost to time. Some say they were scattered across the land, hidden in places where only the worthy could find them. Others believe they sank to the depths of the Arabian Gulf, guarded by the spirits of the sea. Centuries passed, and the tale faded into legend. Until now. Ali, a young historian from Jeddah, had spent years studying ancient manuscripts, searching for any mention of the pearls. When an old sailor, known only as Abu Saqr, claimed to have seen a pearl glowing beneath the waves during a stormy night, Ali knew his journey was about to begin. Guided by cryptic verses and the wisdom of the elders, Ali set sail across the Gulf, tracing the lost pearls through hidden caves, forgotten temples, and abandoned shipwrecks. A secretive group, known as the Brotherhood of the Tide, had sworn to keep the pearls hidden forever, believing their power was too great for any mortal to wield. As Ali uncovered the first pearl, nestled within the ruins of a sunken palace, he felt a pulse of energy course through him. It was as if the sea itself had awakened. The air grew heavy, and the waves roared as if warning him of the trials ahead.
