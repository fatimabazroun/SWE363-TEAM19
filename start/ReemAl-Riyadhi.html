Growing up in Riyadh

Written by: Reem Al-Riyadhi Published by: Ahmad Al-Ahmadi Genre: Personal Stories Region of Origin: Riyadh Word Count: 650

Insights from a youth in the capital city offer a glimpse into the vibrant and evolving life of Riyadh. As one of the fastest-growing cities in the Middle East, Riyadh is a place where tradition and modernity coexist, shaping the experiences of its residents—especially its youth. Growing up in Riyadh means waking up to the call to prayer echoing through the city, a reminder of the deep-rooted Islamic traditions that form the foundation of life here. The mornings are filled with the routine of school, where education is taken seriously as a pathway to success. Schools in Riyadh are diverse, offering both traditional and international curricula, preparing students for a future that blends Saudi heritage with global perspectives. Subjects like Arabic, Islamic studies, and history instill a sense of cultural identity, while science, technology, and foreign languages open doors to international opportunities. Evenings in Riyadh are a mix of social gatherings and leisure activities. The city offers endless options for entertainment, from exploring historical sites like Diriyah to visiting modern landmarks such as the Kingdom Centre Tower. The streets come alive at night, with families dining in traditional restaurants, sipping Arabic coffee, and enjoying the local cuisine. For the younger generation, Riyadh Park and Boulevard City provide a glimpse of the city’s modernization, with international brands, amusement parks, and cultural festivals shaping the urban experience. Sporting activities such as football, a beloved national pastime, bring together communities, whether in neighborhood matches or professional leagues. Despite its rapid transformation, Riyadh remains deeply connected to its traditions. The youth take pride in their cultural heritage, celebrating national events such as Saudi National Day with enthusiasm. Traditional dances like the Ardah are performed, and poetry, an essential part of Saudi culture, is still cherished. The city’s museums and cultural centers, such as the King Abdulaziz Historical Center, serve as reminders of the rich past that continues to inspire younger generations. Family plays an essential role in shaping life in Riyadh. Homes are places of warmth, where gatherings with extended relatives are frequent. Hospitality is a deeply ingrained value, and guests are always welcomed with dates and Arabic coffee. Weddings, religious holidays, and social events bring families together, reinforcing strong communal ties. For many, the city represents ambition and opportunity. With Vision 2030 shaping the country’s future, Riyadh is becoming a hub for innovation, attracting young entrepreneurs and professionals eager to contribute to the nation’s progress. From tech startups to creative industries, the capital city is a place where dreams are nurtured and possibilities seem endless. Government initiatives encourage youth participation in various sectors, and universities like King Saud University and Princess Nourah University equip students with the skills needed to excel in a rapidly changing world. The city is also a melting pot of cultures. As Saudi Arabia becomes more connected to the global stage, Riyadh welcomes expatriates and visitors, creating a dynamic environment where different perspectives meet. Events such as Riyadh Season showcase international entertainment, music, and cuisine, broadening the cultural experiences of its residents. The introduction of cinemas, concerts, and sports events has significantly reshaped the city’s social landscape. However, growing up in Riyadh also comes with challenges. The rapid pace of modernization sometimes creates a tension between tradition and contemporary influences. Youth must navigate expectations from family and society while exploring new aspirations. The city's extreme summer temperatures also shape daily life, with many activities moving indoors to malls and entertainment centers to escape the heat. Growing up in Riyadh is more than just living in a bustling capital—it is about experiencing the fusion of tradition and modernity, embracing cultural heritage while stepping into a promising future. It is a journey shaped by family, faith, and ambition, making Riyadh not just a city, but a way of life.
